Tribute to Shrila Prabhupada

Shrila Prabhupada

Date of Birth: September 1, 1896

Date of Departure: November 14, 1977

Nationality: Indian

Shrila Prabhupada, born Abhay Charan De, was an Indian spiritual teacher and the founder-acharya of the International Society for Krishna Consciousness (ISKCON), commonly known as the Hare Krishna movement. He dedicated his life to spreading the teachings of Bhakti Yoga, the path of devotion to Lord Krishna.

Shrila Prabhupada's vision was to inspire individuals to reawaken their spiritual consciousness and experience the eternal relationship with the Supreme Lord. He aimed to establish a worldwide spiritual society based on the teachings of the Bhagavad Gita and the Shrimad Bhagavatam, ancient scriptures that provide profound insights into the nature of life and the self.

His mission was to disseminate Krishna consciousness and create a global community of devotees dedicated to practicing devotional service. Shrila Prabhupada envisioned a society where people from all walks of life could come together to chant the holy names of Krishna, engage in selfless service, study sacred texts, and promote spiritual values.

Through his tireless efforts, Shrila Prabhupada established over a hundred temples worldwide, translated and authored numerous books, initiated thousands of disciples, and introduced the joyful practice of sankirtan (congregational chanting) to cities across the globe.

Top Two Sanskrit Shlokas:

  1. Verse from Bhagavad Gita:
    "यदा यदा हि धर्मस्य ग्लानिर्भवति भारत।
    अभ्युत्थानमधर्मस्य तदात्मानं सृजाम्यहम्॥"
    (Bhagavad Gita 4.7)
  2. Verse from Shrimad Bhagavatam:
    "हरे नाम हरे नाम
    हरे नामैव केवलम्।
    कलौ नस्त्येव नस्त्येव
    नस्त्येव गतिरन्यथा॥"
    (Shrimad Bhagavatam 12.3.51)
